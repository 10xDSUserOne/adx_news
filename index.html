Impressive Password Statistics to know
Credentials are one of the most sought after and frequently exchanged digital goods in the darknet economy. Many people are still making common password mistakes, such as using their favorite year or using highly popular (and crackable) strings of characters.
A recent study shows that :
 50% of people use the same password for all their logins
51% of people have the same password for their work and personal accounts
Over 80% of data breaches are due to poor password security.
Only 31.3% of internet users update their passwords twice a year

MOVEit body count closes in on 400 orgs, 20M+ individuals
The MOVEit file transfer hack, a massive supply chain attack, has affected a growing number of victims and is considered one of the most significant hacks in recent years. As of July 19, around 383 organizations and over 20 million individuals have been compromised. Notable victims include the US Department of Energy, Shell, Deutsche Bank, EY, PwC, and TJX Companies. ADX data werent impacted with this breach.

LetMeSpy permanently shuts down after data breach
LetMeSpy has formally announced the closure of the company, as it cannot recover from the data breach in June earlier this year. Letmespy's administrators realized on 22 June that a data breach had occurred and notified the Polish Office for Personal Data Protection and filed complaint with law enforcement authorities. Initial digital forensics investigations revealed that hackers not only stole but also deleted personal and sensitive data.

Norway government ministries
uncovered a data attack
The ministries' security and service organization has uncovered a data attack on the ICT platform of 12 ministries. Hackers exploited a zero-day vulnerability in third-party software. The attack affected the ministries' email systems, file servers, and websites. The hackers also stole sensitive data, including email correspondence, financial information, and intellectual property.

Multichain Pauses Operations After USD120 Million Cyber Heist
Blockchain bridge Multichain was hacked on July 6, 2023, and lost up to USD120 million. The hackers were able to exploit a vulnerability in the protocol to steal tokens from the Fantom, Dogechain, and Moonriver blockchains.
